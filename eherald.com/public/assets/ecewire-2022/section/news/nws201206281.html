Mobile operators to expand public access small cells, SK Telecom leads: Informa

Informa Telecoms & Media has issued its latest quarterly small cell market status report which underscores growing operator interest in public access small cells and the femtocell market growth. The report also underlines that small cells to outnumber all macrocells globally during Q4 2012 and that femtocells (aka residential small cells) alone to outnumber all macrocells shortly afterwards in Q1 2013.

In the public access market, the report stated that SK Telecom has successfully started rolling out the world’s first LTE small cell deployment while AT&T, Sprint and China Mobile have all committed to rolling out 3G small cell services – AT&T and Sprint plan to begin later this year. Verizon Wireless also announced its intention to start LTE public access small cells in the future while Sprint is planning to roll out its first LTE designs at the end of the year. All these operators have preferred to proceed with public access services following their successful femtocell offerings.

The femtocell market was recently sustained by the news that Telefónica plans to deploy the technology across its European and South American territories while China Mobile, the world’s largest mobile operator by subscribers, has also begun its own rollout.

Sprint has announced it has quickly accelerated its femtocell deployment to a total of 600,000 units which are up from 250,000 units in 2011. This makes them one of the world’s largest rollouts. Femtocells representing over 80% of the 4.6 million small cells are currently deployed globally across the 41 operator deployments when compared to 5.6 million conventional macrocells. By the close of 2012, the small cells to be 6.4 million and 86% are femtocells. This outnumbered the forecasted 6 million macrocells worldwide. Femtocells to alone outnumber all macrocells during Q1 2013.

“We are starting to see vendor-led public access small cell R&D turning into concrete operator deployment commitments. Over the past few months numerous major operators have announced 3G and LTE public access deployment plans – SK Telecom is undoubtedly leading the charge by already rolling out LTE devices. However, it is notable that all the operators involved in this first wave of deployments have already rolled out femtocell services so are accustomed to small cell challenges and how they can be overcome. With major operator groups such as Telefónica and Telenor preparing widespread femtocell launches in the near future we can expect deployment numbers to quickly rise – especially if they are as committed as Vodafone Group,” said Dimitris Mavrakis, principal analyst at Informa Telecoms & Media.

The reason for growing interest in public access small cells is mainly to offer additional capacity in busy metropolitan hotspots. This is revealed in a recent Small Cell Forum study which found that even a relatively conservative small cell deployment with 4 devices per macrocell would increase typical data rates by over 300% and offload 56% of data. However, public access small cells could also have an impact in rural and developing markets. African operator RascomStar-QAF recently announced it is trialling the technology using satellite backhaul in the Congo and this follows ongoing trials by Vodafone UK and a rural deployment by SoftBank in Japan.

The report also comprises a major industry survey into small cell attitudes. It found that LTE is expected to be the biggest driver for small cells and to be mainly deployed for capacity and coverage in high traffic public areas. The survey also found that small cells are expected to become a mass market phenomenon in 2014 and the important challenges holding them back are backhaul and deployment issues such as power and placement.

The report defines small cells as including femtocells, picocells, microcells and metrocells. Informa Telecoms & Media independently produced this report for the Small Cell Forum and updates on a quarterly basis.
