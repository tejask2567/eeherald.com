Reduce switching loss thru’ asymmetrical parasitic inductance in power conversion circuits

Vincotech presents article about asymmetrical parasitic inductance. High efficiency of power conversion circuits is a design objective on its own. At the top end competing solar inverter manufacturer to develop the most efficient topologies utilizing components with the lowest power dissipation. But high efficiency is also the key for other approaches. The drop in switching losses is the beginning for higher switching frequencies which shows the way to a reduction of the size and weight of the passive components.

The improvement from 96% to 99% efficiency will diminish the effort for cooling by factor four. It is understandable that high efficient circuits are the smartest way to achieve compact design and highest power density. With the utilization of parasitic inductance and consequent execution of basic rules of power electronics is a new power electronics solution based on standard Si components disclosed which extends the traditional designs. The presented new power module concept combines a low inductive turn off with the utilization of the parasitic inductance for a reduction of the turn on losses and the usage of three level switching circuits with the paralleling of fast switching components with components with low forward voltage drop.
