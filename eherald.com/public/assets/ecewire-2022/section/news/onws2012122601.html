New 20MP BSI CMOS image sensor from Toshiba for DSCs

To support capturing high definition video and pictures in low light, Toshiba has launched a new 20-megapixel (MP) CMOS image sensor in the 1/2.3 inch optical format, using backside illumination technology (BSI) to improve sensitivity and imaging performance.

bsl_cmos


Sampling of the new sensor will begin at the end of January 2013 and mass production will follow in August 2013. TCM5115CL achieves 15% improvement in full well capacity—the amount of charge an individual pixel can hold before saturating—against Toshiba's previous generation 16MP sensor (pixel size = 1.34µm).

TCM5115CL is designed to meet the demands of high quality, fast frame rate image capture and HD video recording supporting smooth, slow motion playback, and delivers the high frame rates 60fps at 1080p and 100fps at 720p. With this new product launch, Toshiba is aiming a target of 30% market share in 2015.

Features:
Part Number: TCM5115CL
Optical Format: 1/2.3 inch
Resolution: 20 megapixels
Pixel Size: 1.2 micrometers
Frame Rate: 30fps @ 20M pixel, RAW10
60fps @ 1080P
100fps @ 720P
